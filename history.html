HISTORY

Discovery of the Philippine Islands


Through an expedition, a Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan leading a Spanish fleet discovered and claimed the islands of the Philippines.(March 16,1521)


























Death of Ferdinand Magellan


He was killed by a local ruler, Lapu-Lapu in the Battle in Mactan.(April 27, 1521)



























Naming of the Philippines


During the expedition of Ruy Lopez de Villalobos in 1542–1546, the Philippines was named after King Philip II of Spain.(1543)



























Colonization of the Philippines


The Philippines became Spain's permanent settlement, breaking their way from one island to another. Due to this event, the Philippines underwent Spanish rule which lasted for 333 years.(1565-1898)



























Start of American Rule


The United States governed the Philippines after the American-Spanish War. (1898)



























Philippine-American War


A war seeking for independence was initiated by Filipino nationalists led by Gen. Emilio Aguinaldo—the first president of the Philippines (1899–1901).



























Philippines First Elected Assembly


The First Philippine Assembly was the lower house of the Philippine Legislature established in 1907. It was the first fully elected legislative body in the Philippines. It played a significant role in the country's transition from a colonial territory to a self-governing nation, providing a forum for Filipino political leaders to voice their concerns and shape the direction of the country's governance.(1907)



























The U.S. Promised the Philippines Eventual Independence


In 1916, the United States emphasized its intention to grant independence to the Philippines in the future through the Jones Law or the Philippine Autonomy Act. This was a significant step towards granting greater autonomy to the Filipino government and preparing the Philippines for eventual full independence.(1916)



























Establishment of the Philippine Commonwealth


The establishment of the Philippine Commonwealth in 1935 under President Manuel L. Quezon marked a crucial shift from U.S. territory to self-governing state. It prioritized democratic institution-building, economic stability, and national identity, paving the way for Philippine independence in 1946, the first in Asia.(1935)



























World War II


During World War II, the Philippines endured significant hardship as it became a battleground between Allied and Japanese forces. The brutal occupation led to widespread suffering, including atrocities such as the Bataan Death March. Filipino resistance fighters, alongside Allied forces, eventually liberated the islands, marking a pivotal moment in the nation's history.(1942-1945)



























Philippine Independence


Independence of the Philippines - The Treaty of Manila established the Philippines as a Republic.(1946)



























Martial Law


Pres. Ferdinand Marcos declared Martial law. His regime dictatorship gave him full control over the country. Thousands of citizens died longing for freedom, for their voices to be heard and to regain their rights back. (1972-1981)



























People's Power Revolution


Also known as EDSA (Epifanio de los Santos Avenue) Revolution, millions of Filipinos gathered aiming to overthrow the rule of Pres. Marcos, end his dictatorship and bring back democracy.(February 22-25 1986)
